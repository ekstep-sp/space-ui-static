To foster constant and rapid evolution of innovations, assets would have to be created and used, and should enable collaboration and co-creation among diverse people. Thus, standards play an important role in defining certain requirements and guidelines to make assets accessible, acceptable and easy to consume for all.

These standards are intended for individuals and organisations involved in asset creation and contribution of assets to the SPace Platform.

What is an asset?

An asset is a bundle of resources that can be used repeatedly to provide various benefits. An asset can give ideas, methods, tools, data and connections to imagine and implement a societal mission.

Take the example of an asset that can help in conducting field research. It can have several resources such as a guide (a knowledge resource) which could help the audience with steps to follow in a research process, a software which could help analyse qualitative data and a case study of a field research conducted by an organisation.

Note: Both assets and resources are Content types recognised on the SPace Platform. All Content must follow the terms and policies of SPace Platform, including the Content Policy & Community Guidelines.

General considerations

Asset creators should ensure that the resources are understandable to different individuals (such as social science researchers, technologists, policy makers and programme designers) involved in societal missions. The use of resources will vary depending on the context in which users work. It could be their sector, functions within organisations, their previous knowledge and so on. A policymaker may want to use qualitative data analysis software to analyse policy documents whereas a field researcher may want to use it for transcription analysis. Therefore, the value of each asset must be conveyed with appropriate language, terminology and supporting information.

Guidelines

  1. An asset with a diverse range of resources can help in ensuring the relevance of the asset for all. For example, in the case of an asset on network mapping, some users may only want to understand how to map networks while some others may be interested only in the tool that allows them to map networks. Thus, we recommend that an asset has different types of resources. This can also help in making an asset more valuable for the users. Types of resources including but not limited to:
    1. Knowledge
      • Knowledge resources are containers that store knowledge in the form of articles, method guides, videos, posters and other such learning objects. 
    2. Protocol
      • Protocols, in simple terms, are a set of guidelines and procedures that deal with a particular subject. It gets manifested in terms of documents on standards and specifications. 
      • Protocols provide detailed information on processes and standards for interactions between different entities. It could be APIs, hardware specifications, software documentation or standards for resource creation, among others.
    3. Technology
      • Technology refers to software and hardware. Access to microservices in the form of a codebase is an example of a technology resource. 
    4. Connections
      • Connections are about networks — information about actors and the interactions between them. For instance, an organisation sharing its network of partners can help others to find new actors and leverage existing interactions in the system. 
    5. Data
      • A curated repository of data in an understandable and consumable form, wherever possible, can be critical in making complete use of an asset. Data resources are commonly stored in databases and registries.
  2. An asset becomes easily consumable with different types of knowledge resources. We primarily look at four key types of knowledge resources with each being able to provide a distinct form of engagement for the user.

    4. Example
        • A case study of the asset usage in a particular context helps explain the applicability of the asset. For example, a case study of how an organisation has done strategy mapping will be a valuable resource for an asset on how to do strategy mapping for societal missions.
  3. When users are on the SPace Platform, it is important that they are able to quickly understand what each asset is and what is the value that they provide. Thus, having asset names and descriptions that suggest what one can achieve with that asset becomes critical.
    1. Asset names should be short, preferably 5-6 words, and help a user make sense of what that asset is about.
    2. Asset description should be detailed and more specific than asset name. A suggested practice is to start asset description using below convention:  

      3. <Do What> <On What> <Achieve What>

      • For example, the description of a ‘how-to’ guide on field research can be started as, ‘How to conduct independent field research to get an understanding of the adoption of Societal Platform among users’. 
  4. In order to increase and maintain the shareability of the asset, it becomes essential to ensure clarity of information for each resource and asset. Information can be provided as metadata. 
    1. Name
    2. Description
    3. Thumbnail
    4. Expires on: If the asset is relevant to be on the Platform only for a particular date, an expiry date can be set for the asset. Beyond this date, the asset will not be available on the Platform.
    5. Duration: In case of multimedia assets and resources such as audio, video, duration can tell about the time required to consume an asset. 
    6. Created By (Organisation)
    7. SDG (Sustainable Development Goal)
    8. Catalog: An asset can be further associated with categories and subcategories of the field of Societal Platform. Currently, the following categories and subcategories are supported:

    9. Type: This field is applicable to resources only. The possible values for type are knowledge, protocol, technology, connection, data.
    10. Copyright & Year: If you are an individual, creating original Content, you are the copyright holder. If you are creating this course Content on behalf of an organisation, the organisation may be the copyright holder.  Provide the year of publication on SPace Platform.
    11. Attributions: If you have relied on another work to create this content, provide the name of that creator and the source of that work.
    12. License: This field is applicable to resources only. The possible values for license are dependent on the type of the resource. For knowledge and protocol type, the license values are CC0, CC BY (4.0), CC BY-SA (4.0), CC BY-NC (4.0), CC BY-ND (4.0), CC BY-NC-SA (4.0), CC BY-NC-ND (4.0), Other. For technology type, the license values are MIT, Apache, Other. For data type, the license values are Public Domain, CC0, PDDL, CC BY (4.0), CDLA-Permissive-1.0, ODC-BY, CC BY-SA (4.0), CDLA-Sharing-1.0, ODC-ODbL, CC BY-NC (4.0), CC BY-ND (4.0), CC BY-NC-SA (4.0), CC BY-NC-ND (4.0).
    13. Keywords
    14. Creator: The user who submits the asset on the Platform.
    15. Publisher: The user who reviews and publishes the asset on the Platform.
    16. Format: This field is applicable to knowledge resources only. Some of the possible values of format are article, quiz, webinar-recording, analyst-report etc.
    17. Interface / API: This field is applicable to technology resources only. It can be used to provide the link to the API related to the technology.
    18. Codebase: This field is applicable to technology resources only. It can be used to provide the link to the source code of the technology.
    19. Documentation: This field is applicable to technology resources only. It can be used to share the documentation link for the use of technology.
    20. Sandbox: This field is applicable to technology resources only. It can be used to share the link to a test environment for the technology where other users can experiment and experience it firsthand.
    21. Profile: This field is applicable to connection resources only. This field can be used to provide a public link to the profile of the individual or entity whose information is being shared as a connection.
