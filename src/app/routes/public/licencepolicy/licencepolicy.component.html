License Policy

 

The EkStep  Foundation (the “Foundation”) believes that open Contenti.e. Content that is available free and for public use provides immense opportunities for improving access to scarce resources to create solutions and programs for various societal challenges. Open Content can be reused within the terms of the license and modified within the terms of the license permitted. Source material is given appropriate credit and attribution, in accordance with the terms of its license. Accordingly, the Foundation follows the principles of open Content shared as public goods on its SPace Platform.


Defined terms that are not defined here shall have the meaning ascribed to them
in the Terms of Use.


What are the license options?

Content on SPace can be in the form of a resource (such as knowledge, protocol, technology, connection, data), an asset (i.e. collection of one or more resources), a course (i.e. collection of one or more assets and/or resources). The detailed definition of Content is available in the Terms of Use.


License for resources:

The licenses applicable for different types of resources are as follows:

  1. Knowledge resource OR Protocol resource

    2. Content which is classified as a knowledge or protocol resource on the SPace Platform should preferably to be licensed under the Creative Commons license framework - CC0, CC BY (4.0), CC BY-SA (4.0), CC BY-NC (4.0), CC BY-ND (4.0), CC BY-NC-SA (4.0), CC BY-NC-ND (4.0). Creative Commons licenses allow the creators to retain copyright (except CC0) while allowing others to copy, distribute and make some use of the work under conditions specified for each license type, including attribution. Anyone can use a knowledge or protocol resource  available on the SPace Platform for their work so long as they are adhering to the requirements of the Creative Commons license as chosen by the Creator.


  2. Technology resource

    3. All technology resource should preferably be is licensed under either of the license options below:


    • MIT License  

    A technology resource can be licensed under MIT license which is a permissive-free software license originating at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology.


    • Apache License 

    A technology resource can also be licensed under the Apache license which is a permissive-free software license written by the Apache Software Foundation.


  3. Data resource

    4. All data resources should preferably be licensed under either of the license options below: 

    • Public Domain

    Public Domain Mark:This isn’t technically a license since the dataset owner relinquishes all the rights on the dataset by choosing to dedicate the dataset to the public domain. 


    • CC0

    Creative Commons Public Domain Dedication:This license is one of the open Creative Commons licenses and is like a public domain dedication. It allows the dataset owner to use a license mechanism to surrender the rights on the dataset.


    • PDDL

    Open Data Commons Public Domain Dedication and License:This license is one of the open Creative Commons licenses and is like a public domain dedication. It allows the dataset owner to use a license mechanism to surrender the rights on the dataset.


    • CC BY (4.0)

    Creative Commons Attribution 4.0 International:This license is one of the open Creative Commons licenses and allows users to share and adapt the dataset so long as they give credit to the dataset owner.


    • CDLA-Permissive-1.0

    Community Data License Agreement – Permissive, Version 1.0:This license is one of the Community Data License Agreement licenses and is similar to permissive open source licenses. It allows users to use, modify and adapt the dataset and the data within it, and to share it so long as they give credit to the dataset owner. The CDLA-Permissive terms explicitly do not impose any obligations or restrictions on results obtained from users’ computational use of the data.


    • ODC-BY

    Open Data Commons Attribution License:This license is one of the Open Data Commons licenses and allows users to share and adapt the dataset so long as they give credit to the dataset owner.


    • CC BY-SA (4.0)

    Creative Commons Attribution-ShareAlike 4.0 International:This license is one of the open Creative Commons licenses and allows users to share and adapt the dataset so long as they give credit to the dataset owner and distribute any additions, transformations or changes to the dataset under this license.


    • CDLA-Sharing-1.0

    Community Data License Agreement – Sharing, Version 1.0:This license is one of the Community Data License Agreement licenses and was designed to embody the principles of "copyleft" in a data license. It allows users to use, modify and adapt the dataset and the data within it, and to share the dataset and data with their changes so long as they do so under the CDLA-Sharing and give credit to the dataset owner. The CDLA-Sharing terms explicitly do not impose any obligations or restrictions on results obtained from users’ computational use of the data.


    • ODC-ODbL

    Open Data Commons Open Database License:This license is one of the Open Data Commons licenses and allows users to share and adapt the dataset so long as they give credit to the dataset owner and distribute any additions, transformation or changes to the dataset under this license.


    • CC BY-NC (4.0)

    Creative Commons Attribution-NonCommercial 4.0 International:This license is one of the more restrictive Creative Commons licenses. It allows users to share and adapt the dataset if they give credit to the dataset owner and do not use the dataset for any commercial purposes.


    • CC BY-ND (4.0)

    Creative Commons Attribution-NoDerivatives 4.0 International:This license is one of the more restrictive Creative Commons licenses. It allows users to share the dataset if they give credit to the dataset owner, but they cannot make any additions, transformations or changes to the dataset under this license.


    • CC BY-NC-SA (4.0)

    Creative Commons Attribution-NonCommercial-ShareAlike 4.0 International:This license is one of the most restrictive Creative Commons licenses. It allows users to share the dataset only if they (1) give credit to the dataset owner, (2) do not use the dataset for any commercial purposes, and (3) distribute any additions, transformations or changes to the dataset under this license.


    • CC BY-NC-ND (4.0)

    Creative Commons Attribution-NonCommercial-NoDerivatives 4.0 International:This license is one of the most restrictive Creative Commons licenses. Users can share only the unmodified dataset if they give credit to the dataset owner and do not share it for commercial purposes. Users cannot make any additions, transformations or changes to the dataset under this license.


  4. Connection resource

Content appearing as a connection resource on the SPace Platform doesn’t require any license. However, before a connection resource is published on the SPace Platform, appropriate consent in writing must be taken from the person or entity whose information is being shared as a connection resource.

 

License for asset and course:

All Content appearing as an asset or a course on the SPace Platform is licensed under Creative Commons, CC BY ND 4.0 International License. However, each resource appearing inside an asset or a course is bound by the terms and conditions of their  respective licenses, as applicable.
